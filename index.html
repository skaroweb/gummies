What Are Weight Loss Supplements?

Weight loss supplements are manufactured products, usually in pill, powder, capsule or liquid form, that are meant to be added to your diet to aid weight loss. Common ways that weight loss supplements may claim to work include improving carbohydrate metabolism, increasing fat-burning or energy expenditure and reducing feelings of hunger.

Other weight loss supplements include those that help indirectly by claiming to support your overall health and energy levels and warding off some common effects of dieting, such as fatigue, cravings and potential nutrient deficiencies. Some may include up to a dozen ingredients, ranging from herbs to minerals.

Keep in mind that over-the-counter weight loss supplements are not the same as prescription medications designed to treat people with obesity, known as anti-obesity medications. These medications can be part of the treatment plan for obesity, notes the Obesity Medicine Association, and their safety and efficacy is backed by randomized, clinical trials.

How Effective Are Supplements for Weight Loss?

While there have been studies and bodies of research that have looked into the efficacy of weight loss supplements, most are of low quality, notes a 2020 review in Nutrients. The National Institutes of Health’s Office on Dietary Supplements also states that not only is there a lack of quality research on the efficacy of over-the-counter weight loss supplements, the research that is available on their efficacy often consists of limited data from animal laboratory studies (rather than human clinical trials).

“In almost all cases, additional research is needed to fully understand the safety and/or efficacy of a particular ingredient but some may include ingredients that interfere with certain medications,” states the organization on its website.

Building and maintaining a healthy lifestyle should be the core of any weight loss program, according to Dr. Gupta. “No one can achieve sustainable weight loss without breaking bad habits and developing healthy habits,” he says. There is also no ‘one-size-fits-all’ approach to dieting. This should be personalized to each individual based on their daily routine, eating habits and food preferences.”

Are Weight Loss Supplements Regulated by the FDA?

Over-the-counter weight loss supplements are not regulated by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) as drugs, and do not require premarket review or approval by the FDA, instead placing the responsibility on the supplement manufacturers to ensure that their products are safe and the claims made are truthful and not misleading.

Adarsh Gupta, D.O., director of Valley Forge Weight Management Center in Norristown, Pennsylvania notes that weight loss supplements—which may include over-the-counter medication, herbs and other products that claim weight loss— should be used cautiously. “It is very important for everyone to understand that these supplements are not approved by the FDA,” he says. “The only restrictions that are placed on the supplement is that the supplements cannot claim to cure a disease.”
